Connecting Hk with a mailing list management software

Csaba Hoch Connecting Hk with a mailing list management software [idea, reviewed] <hh/239> (2009-05-18) Children: hh/251, hh/260 
This is a very long-term idea.

Many times it would be handy if the email I got from a heap contained
the heapid of that email. As far as I see there is one way to reach
this goal: by plugging in some part of Hk into the list management
software that manages the mailing list on which the heap is based.

This approach would solve another inconvenience: we would not need the
"IMAP user" whose account is subscribed to the mailing list and is
used to store the emails until they are downloaded by Hk.

They should not be. That's the main reason why I'm hesitant about
giving decent support for moving emails between heaps.

The content of a heapid may change - but if someone bookmarked a post,
it should remain there forever. Just like a page on Wikipedia. It may
change a lot when you go back a year later - but it is still there.

This is a very important principle, which is very strongly advised
also in POSS. (He talks about mailing archives there, but a heap is
basically a mailing archive.)

Csaba Hoch — <hh/262> (2009-05-19) Parent: hh/260 Children: hh/270 
Yet another feature: post pages could have a "show the original" link
that would point to the email of the post (i.e. the original form of
the post [1]) in the mail archive.

This feature could be implemented inside Heapkeeper, but I think
people will trust the "real" mail archive more. Not without reason:
heap maintainers could of course manipulate the "original" post, even
if we don't give them hkshell commands for that.

[1] I'm not sure if I have mentioned this piece of terminology before.
    Email is an immutable object that has been sent by someone and is
    stored in mailboxes; post is a mutable object usually created from
    an email that can be modified and has a history like a wiki page.
